Why this exists

The effort is there. The physiology is not always keeping pace.

Serious athletes and health-focused professionals often reach a point where more effort produces less return. They are not undertrained. They are not lacking motivation. Something physiological is in the way.

A thorough clinical blood review examines the markers behind performance and health: cardiovascular risk, metabolic health, hormonal status, inflammation, and recovery capacity. For some clients the key questions are about training and recovery. For others they are hormonal — gradual changes in energy, body composition, and wellbeing through their 40s and 50s that deserve a proper clinical evaluation, not a brief reassurance that everything looks normal.

Prosper provides that picture, with clinical interpretation and a clear, actionable plan. Where the results call for it, treatment options are discussed properly in a consultation — not issued without context.

George trained in Emergency Medicine before developing a clinical interest in the intersection of physiology, performance, and long-term health. He works with serious amateur athletes and health-focused professionals who want answers that go beyond standard GP blood panels.

Prosper grew from a recurring pattern: patients who trained hard, recovered poorly, and were told their bloods were "normal." The clinical picture was rarely that simple. A deeper look — more markers, proper context, and time to explain what they mean — consistently changed what was possible.

Every Prosper assessment is conducted personally by George. There are no associates, no automated reports, and no outsourced interpretation. The consultation is the product.
