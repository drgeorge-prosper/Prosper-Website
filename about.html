Based in Bath

George trained in Emergency Medicine before developing a clinical interest in the intersection of physiology, performance, and long-term health. That background shaped how he approaches every consultation: quickly, thoroughly, and without unnecessary process.

He works with serious amateur athletes and health-focused professionals who want answers that go beyond a standard GP blood panel. People training consistently but not progressing. People whose energy or body composition has changed in ways that generic advice hasn't addressed. People who simply want a proper, current picture of their long-term health.

Prosper grew from a recurring pattern George saw repeatedly: patients who trained hard, recovered poorly, and were told their bloods were "normal." The clinical picture was rarely that simple. A deeper panel, proper context, and time to actually explain what the numbers meant consistently changed what was possible for that patient.

Every Prosper assessment is conducted personally by George. There are no associates, no automated reports, and no outsourced interpretation. The consultation is the product.

Clinical philosophy

Performance and longevity are the same clinical question, asked at different ages.

Depth over defaults

A standard panel misses the markers that actually explain performance plateaus and gradual decline. Prosper starts from a wider, deeper baseline.

Interpretation, not just data

Numbers on a page are not an answer. The value is in the consultation: context, explanation, and a plan built around your specific physiology.

Alongside your care, not instead of it

Prosper complements your GP and your coach. It never positions itself as a replacement for either.

Our laboratory partner

Why Lola Health

Every Prosper blood panel is processed through Lola Health. This wasn't a default choice. George researched the specialist longevity testing market directly before selecting a laboratory partner, and Lola Health stood out as one of the strongest players in the field: a company built specifically around longevity-grade testing, not a generalist lab offering blood work as one product among many.

That specialism matters. A longevity-focused lab is built around the depth and precision that a performance and health assessment actually needs, rather than the narrower panel a standard GP blood test provides.

Sex hormones

A full hormonal profile, not a single isolated marker. This is where standard testing most often falls short, and where the clinical picture behind energy, recovery, and body composition frequently sits.

ApoB

A more precise marker of cardiovascular risk than standard cholesterol testing alone. Included as standard, not as an optional extra.

70 biomarkers in total

Covering cardiovascular health, metabolic function, inflammation, thyroid function, micronutrient status, and full blood count, alongside the hormonal and lipid detail above.

Testing is done via home venepuncture: a qualified phlebotomist visits you, at a time that suits you, so there is no clinic visit to arrange around your schedule. Results are returned quickly and reliably, ready for Dr Collins to review in full before your consultation.
