Dr George Collins

BSc(Hons) MBChB DipMSK PGDip

Founder, Prosper Performance Medicine

Few UK doctors combine frontline clinical training, elite sport medicine, and a leading role in longevity medicine education. Dr George Collins is one of them, and that combination sits behind every Prosper consultation.

Dr George works in primary care, with a clinical background in emergency medicine that shaped his approach to health: focused on the root cause behind a symptom, not just managing what sits on the surface. A consultation with him treats energy, body composition, cardiovascular health, metabolic health, fitness, recovery, and long term resilience as one connected system, not six separate boxes to tick.

That systems level thinking comes directly from where he practises outside the clinic. Dr George works in elite sport with Bath Rugby, where performance and recovery are monitored daily and nothing is left to guesswork, and helps lead education in the field of longevity medicine through Apex Longevity Academy, keeping him at the sharpest edge of current evidence.

His academic record backs it up. Dr George graduated medical school with distinction and holds an award winning First Class degree in Chemistry, finishing top of his year, the analytical foundation behind every recommendation he makes.

Patients leave a Prosper consultation with a precise, personal understanding of their body and a practical plan to perform better and age well, built by a doctor trusted to do exactly that at the highest level of professional sport.

The same knowledge that protects and optimises elite athletes should not stay locked inside professional sport. It belongs to anyone serious about performing and living at their best.

Clinical philosophy

Performance and longevity are the same clinical question, asked at different ages.

Depth over defaults

A standard panel misses the markers that actually explain performance plateaus and gradual decline. Prosper starts from a wider, deeper baseline.

Interpretation, not just data

Numbers on a page are not an answer. The value is in the consultation: context, explanation, and a plan built around your specific physiology.

Alongside your care, not instead of it

Prosper complements your GP and your coach. It never positions itself as a replacement for either.

Our laboratory partner

Why Lola Health

Every Prosper blood panel is processed through Lola Health. This wasn't a default choice. Dr George researched the specialist longevity testing market directly before selecting a laboratory partner, and Lola Health stood out as one of the strongest players in the field: a company built specifically around longevity-grade testing, not a generalist lab offering blood work as one product among many.

That specialism matters. A longevity-focused lab is built around the depth and precision that a performance and health assessment actually needs, rather than the narrower panel a standard GP blood test provides.

Sex hormones

A full hormonal profile, not a single isolated marker. This is where standard testing most often falls short, and where the clinical picture behind energy, recovery, and body composition frequently sits.

ApoB

A more precise marker of cardiovascular risk than standard cholesterol testing alone. Included as standard, not as an optional extra.

70 biomarkers in total

Covering cardiovascular health, metabolic function, inflammation, thyroid function, micronutrient status, and full blood count, alongside the hormonal and lipid detail above.

Testing is done via home venepuncture: a qualified phlebotomist visits you, at a time that suits you, so there is no clinic visit to arrange around your schedule. Results are returned quickly and reliably, ready for Dr George to review in full before your consultation.
